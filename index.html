Work Project Summary

Project: Data Governance Training and Recognition Program

Objective 📊

To educate HR employees on data governance principles, aligning them with changes in IT policies, data requests, permissions, and service requests, and to promote continuous engagement through a recognition program.

Challenge 🚧

HR employees needed a better understanding of data governance due to the sensitivity and volume of data they handled. Additionally, there was a need to engage employees continuously with data governance practices and recognize adherence to these practices.

Approach 💻

eLearning Module: Developed an interactive course on data governance principles using scenarios, examples, and interactive hotspots.
Real-World Application: Incorporated a Subway lunch order activity to apply data governance concepts in a real-world context.
Recognition Program: Created an ongoing recognition program to reinforce positive behaviors and adherence to data governance practices.

Audience 👥

Several hundred corporate HR employees who handle sensitive data regularly.

Tools & Technologies 🔧
  • Storyline 360: For developing the eLearning course.
  • Microsoft Forms & Power Automate: For building and automating the recognition program.
  • Adobe Illustrator: For designing recognition badges.
  • Power BI: For creating a dashboard to display recognition data.
  • Excel: For data organization and analysis.
  • Microsoft Tools: (Teams, Outlook, Word) For collaboration and communication with stakeholders, reviewers, and recognition program testers.
Solution 📜

The project consisted of three main components:
1. eLearning Course: A course that covered the importance of data governance, key principles, and practical scenarios illustrating the impact of good and bad data management practices.
2. Interactive Learning Experiences: Real-life scenarios and interactive activities, such as the Subway lunch order, to make the learning more relatable and memorable.
3. Recognition Program: An automated, scalable recognition program that allowed HR employees to recognize peers for good data governance practices, with badges, emails, and a public dashboard.

Impact 🎯

Employee Engagement: Thousands of recognitions have been provided through the recognition program. Each monthly HR meeting includes time to discuss recognition and celebrate winners of a random draw who receive gift cards. HR managers and HRBPs have observed a significant shift in how employees engage with data governance, transforming it from a complex topic into one that employees are excited to discuss and address. Employees are now more proactive in reaching out to IT with appropriate questions and solutions to bridge the gap between HRIS and HR practices.
Improvements in Data Governance: The implementation of the data governance training and recognition program has led to a 50% reduction in time spent double-checking, cleaning, and correcting data. Additionally, a cultural shift has occurred where the focus has moved from merely correcting data errors to proactively correcting the processes that cause these errors. This has resulted in more efficient data management and greater transparency between HR and IT processes.
Cost Savings: The custom-built recognition program saved $30k over the alternatively considered option and was implemented using in-house tools, resulting in essentially no additional costs.

Role 🛠

• Conducted the needs assessment and advised on the best delivery options.
• Designed and developed the recognition program, including automating the process, creating badges, and building a dashboard.
• Created the interactive eLearning course, including the real-world application activity.

Timeline 📅

Week 1-2: Scripted and storyboarded the eLearning course.
Week 3-4: Stakeholders reviewed and edited the script.
Week 5-6: Completed the development of the eLearning course, including all reviews.
Month 2: Advised against purchasing a $30k recognition program, researched alternative solutions using existing Microsoft 360 tools.
Month 3: Developed and implemented the custom recognition program.
